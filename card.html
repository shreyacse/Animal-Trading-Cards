Rabbit

Single mane Lionhead rabbits only have one copy of the mane gene

  • Scientific Name: Oryctolagus cuniculus
  • Average Length: 20-50cm
  • Average Lifespan: 3years
  • Habitat: Forests,grasslands,deserts,wetlands

    • Original bred in Belgium, these rabbits made their way to the U.S. in the 1990s and became officially recognized as a stand-alone breed in 2014. Lion head has a wool mane encircling the head, reminiscent of a male lion. Other characteristic traits of the Lionhead include a high head mount, compact upright body type, short well-furred 2" - 3" ears, and weigh usually between 2 1/2 pounds but no more than 3 3/4 pounds.
